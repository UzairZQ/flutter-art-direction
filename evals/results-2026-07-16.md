## What The Tests Demonstrate

- The Design Read, dials, Design Bible, signature idea, motion score, state design, accessibility, and rendered-QA plan appeared consistently.
- High non-genericity did not force high motion or decorative richness.
- The agents selected Flutter primitives based on the interaction instead of defaulting to animation packages.
- Wonderous was treated as a source of continuity and gesture principles rather than a visual template.
- Anti-slop reasoning changed the product structure, not just the color palette.

## What They Do Not Demonstrate

These were planning tests. They do not prove Dart correctness, rendered quality, frame performance, or behavior inside an existing architecture. A release candidate should also be implementation-tested in a disposable Flutter project and visually inspected on target devices.

## Disposable Implementation Check

The motion-led field-guide prompt was also exercised in a fresh Flutter project. A fresh agent produced the theme, tokens, and test plan but stalled before completing the main interaction file; this was not counted as an autonomous pass. The maintainer completed the disposable harness and verified:

- direct upward-drag progress with a settle threshold
- a responsive specimen-to-notes spatial transition using built-in Flutter APIs
- native scrolling after the reveal
- immediate reduced-motion completion
- labeled and Android-sized custom tap targets
- closed and open 390 x 844 rendered golden baselines
- `flutter analyze` with no issues
- four passing widget and golden tests

Visual inspection of the first golden exposed a fixed-offset panel that revealed too much content in the closed state. The offset was changed to derive from actual panel height, the goldens were regenerated, and the full checks passed again. This check demonstrates the value of the skill's rendered-QA requirement; it is evidence for the workflow, not a reusable example app shipped in this repository.
